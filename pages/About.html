Did You Know?

• E-waste usually comes from old TVs, outdated computers, printers, phones, other electronics no longer used.

• E-waste equals 2% of America’s trash in landfills which equals 70% of toxic waste

• Over $60 million in gold/silver are dumped every year

*According to the Environmental Protection Agency



History of E-Waste

Electronic waste, as above, usually comes from cast-offs of televisions, desktops, keyboards, CPUs, printers, etc. In other words, anything that is not used anymore. Most states in the US allow the export of hazardous waste. If you've ever thrown out any digital waste, chances are they are somewhere else in the developing countries.

Something that you probably did not know is that of the 2 million tons of digital products that were disposed of, only 18% was collected for recycling while the other 82% was disposed of in landfills (according to the Environmental Protection Agency)

Returning back to where E-waste usually goes, developed countries such as the US find themselves exporting waste to developing countries such as India, Africa, and China. These countries have tried to prevent the import of e-waste, but soon found out that the laws were not working. China has tried to implement this in 2000, but still has e-waste arriving at their provinces.
